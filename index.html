High School Extracurriculars

While in high school, my interest in art really began to flourish. Before my freshman year, I had particpated in a music camp where we created a band and had a showcase in the end. We got to create our own band tshirts and experiment with many different instruments. My main questions was, "What will we wear?" I created the design for our band, Sternum, t-shirt which we all got to screenprint together. It was alot of fun and after that I would make sketches all the time. My favorite show was Project Runway and I would make my own sketches based on the contestant's weekly challenges. I also gained an interest in business and was able to get on the Executive Board of DECA, the school's entrprenuership and business organization. I'm glad that I aprticipated in this as I got a great scholarship in college for being a part of it.

College Years

When I was deciding where I would go to college, I had two questions:

  1. Is Fashion Merchandising offered as a Major?
  2. Do they have great study abroad programs?
My friends and some family members were not happy about my short checklist for a college. They said things like "You don't care about the sports/party life/sororities??" or "Maybe you should try someothing less specific." I didn't let that stop me and chose a small school in a nice city just 3 hours away from my hometown: Johnson and Wales Unviversity in Charlotte, NC. I learned alot during this time both in and outside of the classroom.
2017 Sharkfest Competition

During my freshman year of college, I won 2nd Place in our school's business pitch competition. Hatima was a monthly subscription based service that would promote small black owned beauty/skin care businesses.This program was a great look into what entrprenuership is and I was honored to be apart of it.

London Study Abroad

During my second study abroad experience, I visited London, England. I had the awesome oppurtunity to intern during London Fashion Week which was so much fun and eye-opening. I was able to see all that goes into a successful fashion show. I really enjoyed being a part of it and know that one day instead of a staff member, I will be a guest at fashion shows.

Post Undergraduate Life and Career Goals

Present Day

Currently, I work as a remote customer service representative for Coach, the luxury leather goods company. It can be very challenging because I am dealing with complaints from disgruntled people. This job has taught me alot about professionalism and conflict resolution. Before making the leap into tech I thought that I would be a Fashion Buyer, but after completing an internship in the field I decided that it wasn't the right career for me. I see my customer service job as a stepping stone towards my future career and not the final destination. I work 10hrs/4days a week so I am able to have 3 whole days to focus on the UX Design bootcamp I'm participating in and my Web Design Master's degree program. I believe in hard work and know that it will pay off in the future. I manage my time wisely because it is my most valuable asset right now.
